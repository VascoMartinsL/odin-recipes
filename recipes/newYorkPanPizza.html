New York-style Pan Pizza

Description:

This is the grandma pizza you encounter in New York. It's just an adaptation of a pizza that existed everywhere in Sicilian homes, wether it be in Chicago, New York City, Texas, or Sicily. It's grandma pizza.

And you can make it in your home even if your're not Sicilian or a grandma.

Recipe:

  • 700g of Sicilian Grandma Dough
  • 180g of low-moisture mozzarella, shredded
  • 110g fresh mozzarella
  • Dried Sicilian Oregano
  • 102g Spicy grandma Sauce
  • 20g Sicilian anchovy fillets, optional.
  • 25g Pecorino-Romano, grated
  • 30g extra-virgin olive oil

Steps:

  1. Preheat the oven with pizza stones to 250 degrees Celcius.
  2. Put pizza in a pan and spread the dough to every corner
  3. Top the pizza with the low-moisture mozzarella. Go all the way to the edge
  4. Using your fingers, pinch off coin sized pieces of fresh mozzarella and evenly disperse onto pizza. Dust with the oregano.
  5. Using a spoon, spread the spicy tomato sauce. Add the anchovies evenly starting from the outside in.
  6. Bake the pizza directly on the pizza stone for 15 to 20 minutes, until the crust is golden brown.
  7. Remove the pizza from the pan with a spatula and transfer to a coolinng rack. Top with Pecorino-Romano and drizzle with the olive oil
  8. Allow 5 minutes before cutting.
